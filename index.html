§ 01 · About

An engineer working at the seam where hardware meets simulation.

I'm a Robotics and Automation engineer with a research-led, applied background in robot simulation, offline programming (OLP), virtual commissioning, and mechanical design. I enjoy owning a problem from CAD and cell layout all the way through motion planning, collision checks, and onsite commissioning.

My graduate research at OvGU Magdeburg — in association with Volkswagen AG — focused on PPR-based digital factory modelling and industrial bin-picking optimisation. On the industrial side, I've spent the last year and a half at Strama-MPS programming and commissioning KUKA cells for Body-in-White, automotive, e-mobility, and medical-electronics lines to industrial standards.

I care about the unglamorous details — cycle time, reach, clearance, safe zones, and the twenty small decisions that decide whether a robot cell works on day one or week four. Outside of work I travel, cook, hike, read , watch anything from animes to documentaries and of course doomscroll Instagram.

03 /

Master Thesis — Robot Simulation Methodology for Bin Picking

In association with Volkswagen AG, Wolfsburg: optimised industrial bin picking through a Lean Simulation Methodology and Product–Process–Resource (PPR) approach. Built the backend simulation in RoboDK and Python for collision detection and reachability, plus a custom UI for 3D visualisation of bin-picking data. Platform based on Universal Robots and CAD.

Universal Robots RoboDK Python PPR Modelling Lean Simulation CAD
2023 Thesis · Volkswagen AG
04 /

Industry 4.0 Cyber-Physical Control System

Realisation of a model-based, Industry 4.0 control system using WAGO PLC modules, IEC 61131 programming, OPC-UA, and AutomationML. The project formalised the system through Asset Administration Shells (AAS) and Model-Based Systems Engineering, creating a reusable cyber-physical architecture across manufacturing stations.

WAGO PLC IEC 61131 OPC-UA AutomationML AAS MBSE
2022 — 2023 Research · OvGU
05 /

Zero-Gravity Airplane — VDI Requirements Engineering

Requirements-engineering-based conceptual design for a zero-gravity research aircraft, developed against German VDI standards. Captured stakeholder needs, derived functional and non-functional requirements, traced them through a systems architecture and developed and analysed CAD Models.

Requirements Engg. VDI Standards MBSE CAD/CAE
2021 — 2022 Academic · OvGU
06 /

Collaborative Robot Operational Concepts (UR)

Development of industrial operational concepts around Universal Robots cobots — including gripper conceptualisation, robot motion planning, and safety-aware workflows. Part of broader work on lean, human-adjacent manufacturing cells.
